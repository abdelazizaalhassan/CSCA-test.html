Lines — Slope, Inclination Angle & Equations

Slope and Inclination Angle — The Connection

Every non-vertical line has a slope m and an inclination angle α ∈ [0°, 180°). They are connected by:

m = tan α      (α is the angle from positive x-axis, measured counterclockwise)

α = 90° → vertical line → slope undefined
α = 0° or 180° → horizontal line → slope = 0

Common values: tan 30° = √3/3  ·  tan 45° = 1  ·  tan 60° = √3  ·  tan 120° = −√3  ·  tan 150° = −√3/3
Inclination Angle from Equation — Key Steps For y = √3·x + 10: slope m = √3. Since tan α = √3 and α ∈ [0°,180°) → α = 60°.
For x + √3·y − 2 = 0: rearrange → y = −(1/√3)x + 2/√3. Slope m = −1/√3 = −√3/3.
tan α = −√3/3 and α ∈ [0°,180°) → α = 150° (Q2 angle gives negative tan).

Circles — Standard Form, General Form & Completing the Square

Two Forms of the Circle Equation

Standard Form (Center-Radius)
(x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²

Center: (h, k)   Radius: r

Watch the signs! (x+3)²+(y−2)²=9
Center: (−3, +2), NOT (+3,−2).
Radius: √9 = 3.
General Form
x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0

Complete the square to find center and radius:
r² = (D/2)² + (E/2)² − F

x²+y²+2x−2y=0:
r² = 1+1−0 = 2 → r=√2
Sign Trap — The Most Common Circle Error The standard form is (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r². The center is (+h, +k).
For (x + 3)²: this equals (x − (−3))² → h = −3, not +3.
For (y − 2)²: this equals (y − 2)² → k = +2.
Center of (x+3)²+(y−2)²=9 is (−3, 2), radius = 3.

Lesson 3.4

Ellipses & Hyperbolas

Ellipse — Standard Forms

Foci on x-axis: x²/a² + y²/b² = 1   (a > b > 0)
Foci on y-axis: x²/b² + y²/a² = 1   (a > b > 0)

c² = a² − b²   (where c = focal distance from center)
Foci: (±c, 0) if foci on x-axis; (0, ±c) if foci on y-axis
Eccentricity: e = c/a   (0 < e < 1 for ellipse)
Sum of distances from any point on ellipse to two foci = 2a
Which Denominator Is Larger? → That Axis Has the Foci x²/4 + y²/20 = 1: a²=20 (under y²) → foci on y-axis at (0, ±√(20−4)) = (0, ±4).
x²/25 + y²/16 = 1: a²=25 (under x²) → foci on x-axis at (±√(25−16), 0) = (±3, 0).

Rule: foci go toward the larger denominator's axis.

Hyperbola — Standard Forms

Foci on x-axis: x²/a² − y²/b² = 1
Foci on y-axis: y²/a² − x²/b² = 1

c² = a² + b²   (NOTE: PLUS, not minus — opposite of ellipse!)
Foci: (±c, 0) or (0, ±c)
Eccentricity: e = c/a   (e > 1 for hyperbola)
Hyperbola vs Ellipse — The Critical Difference Ellipse: x²/a² + y²/b² = 1  → c² = a² − b²  (subtract)
Hyperbola: x²/a² − y²/b² = 1  → c² = a² + b²  (add)

Also: for a hyperbola to exist, m and n in mx²+ny²=1 must have opposite signs → mn < 0.

Vectors & Complex Numbers

Vector Operations

If a⃗ = (a₁, a₂) and b⃗ = (b₁, b₂):
Addition: a⃗ + b⃗ = (a₁+b₁, a₂+b₂)
Subtraction: a⃗ − b⃗ = (a₁−b₁, a₂−b₂)
Scalar multiple: k·a⃗ = (ka₁, ka₂)
Dot product: a⃗·b⃗ = a₁b₁ + a₂b₂
Magnitude: |a⃗| = √(a₁²+a₂²)
Midpoint Vector Formula O is origin. A, B are points on the plane. P is midpoint of AB.
Vector OP⃗ = (OA⃗ + OB⃗) / 2 = ½(OA⃗ + OB⃗)

This is because the midpoint coordinates average the endpoints' coordinates.

Complex Numbers — Operations & Geometry

z = a + bi where i² = −1

Addition: (a+bi)+(c+di) = (a+c)+(b+d)i
Multiplication: (a+bi)(c+di) = (ac−bd)+(ad+bc)i
Division: (a+bi)/(c+di) = [(a+bi)(c−di)] / (c²+d²)

Modulus: |z| = √(a²+b²)
Conjugate: z̄ = a − bi

On complex plane: real part = x-coordinate, imaginary part = y-coordinate
Powers of i — Cycle of 4 i¹=i   i²=−1   i³=−i   i⁴=1   i⁵=i   (repeats every 4)
To find iⁿ: divide n by 4, look at remainder.
i¹⁰⁰ = (i⁴)²⁵ = 1²⁵ = 1  ·  i³⁰ = (i⁴)⁷·i² = 1·(−1) = −1
